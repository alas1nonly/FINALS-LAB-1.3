Gabriel "Hev Abi" Abilla

Hev Abi, an accomplished rapper and songwriter, was born on January 31, 2002, in Quezon City, Philippines. Raised by his parents, Mr. and Mrs. Abilla, in the vibrant Tomas Morato area, he grew immersed in local hip-hop culture alongside supportive peers. Hev Abi launched his music journey with the 2020 single “Pusong Bato,” followed by EPs like Kwentong Jimenez in 2021 and Sakred Boy in 2022. He gained massive traction in 2023 with viral hits such as “WELCOME2DTQ,” “Para sa Streets,” and “Lil Kasalanan Shortie.” Co-founding Downtown Q Entertainment, he collaborated with artists like Kristina Dawn and gins&melodies. His debut album Kung Alam Mo Lang dropped in 2023, exploring vulnerability and love. In 2024, he released the mixtape bahay namin maliit lamang and held his sold-out solo concert Morato Most Wanted. He became the first local act with eight simultaneous tracks on Philippines Songs charts and topped Spotify’s male OPM artists. Hev Abi, a dedicated rapper and songwriter, maintains a private life focused on music and Quezon City roots. He draws inspiration from family support and friendships, with no public details on romantic partnerships or children. His tattoos and lyrics often pay homage to his hometown, balancing rising fame with grounded connections. Quezon City figures heavily in his music. Hev Abi said he always pays tribute to his hometown and Tomas Morato, like the song "QC Girls." Even his first major concert held at New Frontier Theater in Cubao last 2024 was titled "Morato Most Wanted" According to Scout Magazine, Hev Abi looks up to Ye (formerly known as Kanye West), Tyler, the Creator, and Pharrell. He is also inspired by Filipino producers like NJ and Pxyche.
